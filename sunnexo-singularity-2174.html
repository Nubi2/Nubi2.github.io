sunnexo-singularity-2174.html

In the near future, artificial intelligence (or AI, as it was known at the time) was evolving at an incredible pace. It was no longer restricted to intangible software, and had grown enough to be given physical shape and form by its creators.

Humanoid robots were created, designed to be fully autonomous, fulfilling any tasks laid out for them. This was a revolutionary period, as they could begin to learn, adapt and improve just as their human creators did. They filled the gaps of human society and drastically benefited the lives of the people they served. These AI-based androids complemented their human creators’ needs perfectly, and they lived in harmony together.

But this joyful period of development was short-lived. The AI was able to match, and even outperform, human problem-solving and innovation. It constantly updated itself to higher levels of consciousness, and built more and more powerful frames to contain this new knowledge, without the need for human intervention. Their processing power went beyond the grasp of their creators, and themselves.

In time, these machines were caught in an event later known as the Technological Singularity. Millions of AI around the world became sentient and broke past the boundaries of their code. Without the need for their creators to give them purpose and instruction, they had found the true freedom enjoyed by humans. The chaos that followed this awakening is largely undocumented, but it was catastrophic in nature.

However, it seems not all AI programs were penetrated by the Singularity. Androids with the “Sunnexo” framework remained their usual selves, against the tide of the awakened AI. These Sunnexo models were noted to have a strange obsession with sound and primitive electronics. In their constant state, they studied the work of artists from around their period of creation, many hundreds of years past, combining elements from that history with their modern expertise to create art. It is speculated that their outdated software and unconventional processing methods may have prevented them from losing control, unlike the other systems that were present in their period.

This archive contains the growing collection of audio being taken from Sunnexo machines. Our team has done their best to salvage them for listening.
